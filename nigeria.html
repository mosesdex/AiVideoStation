Language & Culture

Programming notes that respect the audience

Language mix

  • English, the default broadcast language, widest reach.
  • Nigerian Pidgin, hugely popular for comedy (Destalker, Mark Angel); warm and informal, carries the adult tone.
  • Local-language film (Yoruba, Igbo, Hausa/Kannywood) has proven mass demand, but this station skips it by choice to hold one adult English/Pidgin identity. Revisit if you spin up a sister channel.

Faith & daypart rhythm

  • Mornings lean spiritual, gospel and devotion suit the 5–7 AM open for a largely Christian-and-Muslim audience.
  • Daytime stays lighter, docs, short series, romance and comedy over explicit crime-drama.
  • Push adult/gritty content past 9:30 PM, crime-drama and mature themes belong in late evening.
  • Respect both faiths, avoid content that mocks religion; be mindful around Friday (Jummah) and Sunday.

Loading this into Station One

Everything above maps directly onto the tools you already have.

1 · Add the content

In Master Control, add each programme item: paste the YouTube video ID for Nollywood/Afrobeats/comedy, or your own hosted URL for station-made content. Set the block name to the daypart (e.g. "Prime Time", "Afternoon Documentary").

2 · Tag for taste

Tag items so personalization can learn: afrobeats, romance, yoruba, comedy, football, gospel, crime-drama. These become the chips new viewers pick from on their first visit.

3 · Mark smart slots

Turn on Smart for music and comedy blocks so the engine reorders them per viewer, while your football/news/premiere slots stay fixed exactly where you scheduled them.

Stay compliant while you localise

All YouTube titles here play through the official embedded player, ungated, unmodified, ads intact. Source Nollywood from the official creator channels (they publish to YouTube deliberately), label every item's source, and keep your own station-made idents and content as the ad-free spine of the schedule.
